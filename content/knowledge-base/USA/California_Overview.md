## üìã Statutory Framework Overview

### **California Health & Safety Code - Division 2: Licensing Provisions**

**Chapter 3: California Community Care Facilities Act (Sections 1500-1567.94)**
- General provisions for ALL community care facilities
- Licensing authority and procedures
- Enforcement and penalties
- Applies to: ARF, Group Homes, Child Care Centers, Foster Family Homes

**Chapter 3.2: Residential Care Facilities for the Elderly (Sections 1569-1569.889)**
- Specific provisions for RCFE only
- 9 articles covering all aspects of RCFE regulation
- Separate chapter due to unique elderly care requirements

**Chapter 3.4: Child Day Care Act (Sections 1596.70-1597.68)**
- Specific provisions for child care facilities
- Day care centers and family child care homes

### **California Code of Regulations - Title 22: Social Security**

**Division 6: Licensing of Community Care Facilities**
- Chapter 1: General Requirements (applies to all facilities)
- Chapter 2: Adult Residential Facilities (ARF)
- Chapter 3: Family Day Care Homes
- Chapter 4: Small Family Homes (foster care)
- Chapter 5: Group Homes (children's residential)
- Chapter 6: Adult Day Programs
- Chapter 7: Adoption Agencies
- Chapter 8: Residential Care Facilities for the Elderly (RCFE)

**Division 12: Child Care Facility Licensing Regulations**
- Chapter 1: Child Care Center General Licensing Requirements
- Chapter 3: Family Day Care Homes for Children

### **California Welfare & Institutions Code**

**Division 4.5: Services for the Developmentally Disabled (Lanterman Act)**
- Sections 4500-4906
- Regional Center system
- Rights and services for people with developmental disabilities
- Applies to ARF and Group Homes serving DD population

**Division 9: Public Social Services**
- Foster care and child welfare system
- Applies to Group Homes and foster care placements

## üìä Key Insights & Findings

### **California's Comprehensive Residential Care System**

California operates one of the most comprehensive residential care regulatory systems in the United States, with distinct frameworks for different populations:

1. **Age-Based Differentiation:**
   - Children (0-17): Group Homes, STRTP, specialized facilities
   - Adults (18-59): ARF with various specializations
   - Elderly (60+): RCFE with levels of care

2. **Need-Based Specialization:**
   - Developmental disabilities: Served across all age groups with Regional Center coordination
   - Mental health: Specialized facilities for both children and adults
   - Physical disabilities: Adaptive facilities across age groups
   - Medical fragility: Enhanced care in specialized settings

3. **Unified Licensing Structure:**
   - All facilities regulated by CDSS Community Care Licensing Division
   - Common application forms (LIC 200, LIC 281, LIC 215)
   - Shared Title 22 regulatory framework
   - Consistent enforcement and inspection processes

4. **Specialized Oversight:**
   - STRTP: Dual licensing (CDSS + DHCS mental health approval)
   - EBSH: Enhanced requirements for behavioral support
   - Regional Centers: Coordination for DD services
   - County child welfare: Oversight of foster care placements

### **Regulatory Complexity by Facility Type**

**Most Complex:** STRTP (Short-Term Residential Therapeutic Programs)
- Dual licensing requirements (CDSS + DHCS)
- Mental health program approval
- QRTP accreditation
- Integrated services model
- Intensive staffing and training requirements

**Moderate Complexity:** RCFE with Dementia Care, ARF-EBSH
- Enhanced staffing ratios
- Specialized training requirements
- Behavioral support planning
- Additional safety measures

**Standard Complexity:** Basic RCFE, Standard ARF, Basic Group Homes
- Standard licensing requirements
- Regular staffing ratios
- Core program requirements

### **Common Requirements Across All Facility Types**

1. **Licensing Process:**
   - LIC 200 application
   - Background checks (DOJ, FBI)
   - Facility inspection
   - Capacity determination
   - Annual licensing fees

2. **Staffing:**
   - Administrator qualifications
   - Staff-to-resident/client ratios
   - Background clearances
   - Training requirements (CPR, First Aid, facility-specific)

3. **Physical Plant:**
   - Building codes and fire safety
   - Accessibility (ADA compliance)
   - Room size and occupancy limits
   - Kitchen and food service standards
   - Emergency exits and evacuation plans

4. **Resident/Client Rights:**
   - Personal rights and dignity
   - Privacy and confidentiality
   - Freedom from abuse and neglect
   - Participation in care planning
   - Complaint and grievance procedures

5. **Program Requirements:**
   - Individual care/service plans
   - Activities and socialization
   - Health monitoring
   - Medication management (if applicable)
   - Emergency preparedness

6. **Documentation:**
   - Resident/client records
   - Incident reports
   - Staff records and training logs
   - Medication logs
   - Inspection reports

### **Unique California Features**

1. **Regional Center System:**
   - 21 Regional Centers statewide
   - Serve people with developmental disabilities from birth through life
   - Fund and coordinate services
   - Person-Centered Planning approach
   - Unique to California (most states use different DD service models)

2. **Continuum of Care Reform (CCR):**
   - Major child welfare reform (2015)
   - Shifted from group homes to family-based care
   - Created STRTP model for intensive treatment
   - Reduced use of congregate care
   - Emphasis on trauma-informed care

3. **RCFE Levels of Care:**
   - Tiered system based on resident needs
   - Level 1-4 designations
   - Enhanced staffing for higher levels
   - Allows aging in place with increasing needs

4. **Enhanced Behavioral Support Homes (EBSH):**
   - Specialized ARF for adults with DD and challenging behaviors
   - 1:2 or 1:3 staffing ratios
   - Board-certified behavior analysts
   - Positive behavior support plans
   - Unique California model
