## Executive Summary

Illinois operates a **dual-track residential care system** with distinct regulatory frameworks for Assisted Living Establishments (ALE) and Supportive Living Facilities (SLF). This bifurcated structure reflects Illinois's commitment to both private-pay market flexibility and Medicaid-funded supportive living innovation.

**Key Characteristics:**

Illinois's regulatory approach distinguishes between **Assisted Living Establishments** (regulated under 77 IAC Part 295 and 210 ILCS 9/) serving primarily private-pay residents, and **Supportive Living Facilities** (regulated under 89 IAC Part 146 and 305 ILCS 5/5-5.01a) serving Medicaid-eligible individuals through an innovative alternative to nursing home placement. This dual structure enables market segmentation while promoting aging in place across payment sources.

The **Illinois Department of Public Health (IDPH)** regulates Assisted Living Establishments and Shared Housing Establishments, bringing public health expertise to residential care oversight. The **Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services (HFS)** certifies Supportive Living Facilities as Medicaid providers, creating dual oversight for facilities serving both private-pay and Medicaid populations.

Illinois's **Supportive Living Program (SLP)** represents one of the nation's most successful Medicaid-funded assisted living initiatives, serving thousands of individuals who would otherwise require nursing home placement. The program's emphasis on apartment-style living, resident autonomy, and person-centered services has influenced assisted living policy nationally.

The state's **apartment-style requirement** for Assisted Living Establishments (single-occupancy private units) distinguishes Illinois from states permitting shared rooms, reflecting consumer preference for privacy and independence. Shared Housing Establishments provide an alternative for residents preferring or requiring shared accommodations.

## Regulatory Structure

### Dual Agency Authority

**Illinois Department of Public Health (IDPH)** regulates Assisted Living Establishments and Shared Housing Establishments through its Division of Assisted Living. IDPH manages licensing, inspection, complaint investigation, enforcement, and policy development for assisted living statewide.

**Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services (HFS)** certifies and oversees Supportive Living Facilities as Medicaid providers. HFS establishes program standards, reimbursement rates, quality assurance requirements, and compliance monitoring for SLP facilities.

The dual agency structure creates complexity for facilities serving both private-pay and Medicaid populations, requiring compliance with both IDPH licensure standards and HFS certification requirements. ### Legislative and Regulatory Framework

**Assisted Living Statutory Authority:**

**210 ILCS 9/ Assisted Living and Shared Housing Act** establishes IDPH's authority to license and regulate assisted living establishments and shared housing establishments. The Act emphasizes:
- Flexibility to allow residents to age in place
- Resident autonomy and choice
- Homelike environments
- Person-centered services
- Protection of resident rights

**Administrative Regulations:**

**77 IAC Part 295 - Assisted Living and Shared Housing Establishment Code** provides comprehensive regulatory standards covering:
- Licensure procedures and requirements
- Administrator qualifications
- Physical plant standards
- Staffing requirements
- Service delivery standards
- Resident rights and protections
- Health and safety requirements
- Medication administration
- Food service and nutrition
- Emergency preparedness

**Supportive Living Statutory Authority:**

**305 ILCS 5/5-5.01a** establishes the Supportive Living Program within Illinois's Medicaid program, creating an alternative to nursing home placement for eligible individuals.

**Administrative Regulations:**

**89 IAC Part 146 - Supportive Living Program** establishes certification standards for SLP facilities including:
- Certification procedures and requirements
- Physical environment requirements (apartment-style living)
- Service requirements and service plans
- Staffing and personnel standards
- Resident rights and autonomy
- Quality assurance and compliance monitoring
- Reimbursement methodology

## Facility Types and Definitions

### Assisted Living Establishments (ALE)

**Definition:** Assisted Living Establishments are facilities or portions of facilities that provide housing, personal care services, and intermittent nursing services for three or more adults unrelated to the owner or operator.

**Statutory Authority:** 210 ILCS 9/ and 77 IAC Part 295

**Distinguishing Feature:** ALEs must provide **single-occupancy private apartment units** with individual bathrooms, kitchenettes, and lockable doors. This apartment-style requirement distinguishes Illinois from many states permitting shared rooms.

**Capacity:** Minimum three residents; no statutory maximum

**Resident Population:** ALEs serve adults who:
- Require assistance with activities of daily living
- Need supervision and monitoring
- Do not require continuous nursing care
- Are generally ambulatory or require limited mobility assistance
- Can participate in emergency evacuation with staff assistance
- Prefer private apartment-style living

**Service Scope:** Assisted Living Establishments provide:
- Private apartment-style housing with kitchenettes and bathrooms
- Three meals daily (or meal preparation assistance)
- Personal care services (bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, mobility)
- Medication administration or supervision
- Intermittent nursing services
- 24-hour supervision and monitoring
- Social and recreational activities
- Housekeeping and laundry services
- Emergency response systems
- Coordination with healthcare providers

**Intermittent Nursing Services:** ALEs may provide intermittent nursing services including:
- Health assessments and monitoring
- Medication management oversight
- Wound care and treatments
- Coordination with physicians
- Staff training and supervision

Nursing services must be provided by or under the supervision of licensed nurses (RN or LPN).

**Aging in Place:** Illinois regulations emphasize flexibility to allow residents to age in place, enabling ALEs to serve residents with increasing care needs through enhanced services and supports.

**Licensing:** ALEs require IDPH licensure with annual renewal.

### Shared Housing Establishments (SHE)

**Definition:** Shared Housing Establishments are facilities or portions of facilities that provide housing, personal care services, and intermittent nursing services for three or more adults unrelated to the owner or operator, **without the requirement for private single-occupancy units**.

**Statutory Authority:** 210 ILCS 9/ and 77 IAC Part 295

**Distinguishing Feature:** SHEs may provide shared accommodations (double or multiple occupancy rooms) rather than private apartments, offering a more affordable alternative to ALEs.

**Service Scope:** SHEs provide the same services as ALEs but in shared living arrangements rather than private apartments.

**Licensing:** SHEs require IDPH licensure with annual renewal under the same regulatory framework as ALEs (77 IAC Part 295).

### Supportive Living Facilities (SLF)

**Definition:** Supportive Living Facilities are residential settings that provide apartment-style housing, 24-hour on-site staff, personal care services, and intermittent nursing services to Medicaid-eligible individuals as an alternative to nursing home placement.

**Statutory Authority:** 305 ILCS 5/5-5.01a and 89 IAC Part 146

**Program Purpose:** The Supportive Living Program (SLP) serves individuals who:
- Are Medicaid-eligible
- Require nursing home level of care (as determined by assessment)
- Can be appropriately served in supportive living settings
- Choose SLP over nursing home placement

**Facility Configuration:** SLFs must be either:
1. Free-standing facilities, OR
2. Distinct physical and operational entities within mixed-use buildings

SLFs must maintain clear separation from nursing homes or other healthcare facilities to preserve the residential character.

**Apartment Requirements:** SLFs must provide **private apartment-style units** containing:
- Private bathroom with toilet, sink, and bathing facility
- Kitchenette with sink, refrigerator, and cooking appliance
- Lockable entrance door (resident controls access)
- Individual climate controls
- Emergency call system
- Adequate square footage for living and sleeping areas
- Storage for personal belongings

**Service Philosophy:** SLP emphasizes:
- Resident autonomy and choice
- Aging in place
- Homelike environments
- Person-centered care
- Community integration
- Maximum independence

**Service Scope:** SLFs provide:
- Private apartment-style housing
- 24-hour on-site staffing
- Personal care services (unlimited as needed)
- Intermittent nursing services
- Medication administration and management
- Three meals daily (or meal preparation assistance)
- Social and recreational activities
- Housekeeping and laundry services
- Emergency response systems
- Transportation assistance
- Coordination with healthcare providers and community services

**Staffing Requirements:** SLFs must provide:
- 24-hour awake staff presence
- Licensed and certified staff sufficient to meet resident needs
- Nursing services by or under supervision of licensed nurses
- Personal care provided by trained staff
- Administrator meeting qualification requirements

**Medicaid Reimbursement:** SLP facilities receive Medicaid reimbursement covering:
- Room and board
- Personal care services
- Nursing services
- Medication administration
- Activities and programming
- Care coordination

Reimbursement rates vary by region and resident acuity level.

**SSI Rate Acceptance:** SLP facilities must accept the Supplemental Security Income (SSI) rate for Medicaid residents, ensuring affordability and access.

**Apartment Reservation:** SLP facilities must reserve at least **25% of apartments** for individuals receiving SSI or with income below specified thresholds, promoting economic diversity.

**Certification:** SLFs require HFS certification in addition to IDPH licensure (if also operating as ALE). Certification is renewed periodically based on compliance with program standards.

**Dementia Care Settings:** SLP facilities may apply for certification as Dementia Care Settings, authorizing specialized services for residents with dementia including enhanced staffing, specialized training, environmental modifications, and dementia-specific programming.

## Licensing and Certification Requirements

### Assisted Living Establishment Licensure

**Application Process:**

Prospective operators must submit comprehensive applications to IDPH including:

1. **Application Forms:** Complete IDPH licensing application forms with all required information

2. **Operator Information:** Ownership structure, corporate organization, and operator qualifications

3. **Administrator Designation:** Identification of proposed administrator with qualifications documentation

4. **Physical Plant Documentation:**
   - Facility location and address
   - Architectural plans showing apartment layouts
   - Life Safety Code compliance documentation
   - Accessibility compliance verification
   - Certificate of occupancy

5. **Operational Plans:**
   - Staffing plans and schedules
   - Service delivery models
   - Admission and discharge criteria
   - Emergency preparedness plans
   - Food service plans

6. **Policies and Procedures:** Comprehensive operational policies addressing all regulatory requirements

7. **Background Screening:** Criminal history checks for owners, administrators, and staff

**Pre-Licensure Inspection:** IDPH conducts pre-licensure inspections verifying physical plant compliance, operational readiness, and regulatory compliance.

**Initial License Issuance:** Upon satisfactory completion of pre-licensure inspection and application review, IDPH issues initial operating licenses.

**License Renewal:** ALE licenses must be renewed **annually**. Renewal requires submission of renewal applications, payment of fees, and demonstration of continued compliance.

**License Fees:** Illinois charges license fees based on facility capacity. Fee schedules are available on the IDPH website.

### Supportive Living Facility Certification

**Certification Application:**

Facilities seeking SLP certification must submit applications to HFS including:

1. **Certification Application Forms:** Complete HFS certification application forms

2. **Facility Information:** Physical plant documentation demonstrating compliance with apartment requirements

3. **Medicaid Provider Enrollment:** Enrollment as Medicaid provider

4. **Service Capacity:** Documentation of capacity to serve Medicaid-eligible residents requiring nursing home level of care

5. **Staffing Plans:** Demonstration of adequate staffing to meet SLP requirements

6. **Policies and Procedures:** SLP-specific policies addressing person-centered care, resident autonomy, and program requirements

**Certification Inspection:** HFS conducts certification inspections verifying compliance with SLP standards.

**Certification Issuance:** Upon satisfactory completion of certification inspection and application review, HFS issues SLP certification.

**Certification Renewal:** SLP certification is renewed periodically based on continued compliance with program standards.

**Dementia Care Setting Certification:** Facilities seeking Dementia Care Setting designation must submit additional applications demonstrating specialized capabilities including enhanced staffing, specialized training, environmental modifications, and dementia-specific programming.

### Administrator Qualifications

**Assisted Living Establishment Administrators:**

**Age Requirement:** Minimum 21 years of age

**Qualifications:** Administrators must possess ONE of the following:

1. **Licensed Registered Nurse (RN)** or **Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN)** from Illinois Department of Financial and Professional Regulation

2. **Bachelor's degree** in healthcare administration, social work, nursing, gerontology, or related field

3. **Associate degree** in healthcare administration, social work, nursing, gerontology, or related field PLUS one year experience in healthcare or human services

4. **High school diploma or equivalent** PLUS two years experience in healthcare or human services

**Training:** Administrators must complete IDPH-approved administrator training addressing:
- Illinois assisted living regulations
- Resident rights and advocacy
- Service planning and delivery
- Medication administration oversight
- Staffing and personnel management
- Emergency preparedness
- Quality assurance

**Background Screening:** All administrators must pass criminal background checks

**Continuing Education:** Administrators must complete continuing education requirements to maintain current knowledge

**Supportive Living Facility Administrators:**

SLF administrators must meet the same qualifications as ALE administrators, with additional training on SLP requirements, person-centered care, and Medicaid program compliance.

## Staffing Requirements

### General Staffing Standards

Illinois regulations require facilities to maintain **adequate staffing** to meet resident needs and ensure health, safety, and welfare. Staffing adequacy is determined by:
- Number of residents and their individual needs
- Resident acuity levels and service requirements
- Physical layout of facility
- Time of day and day of week
- Services provided

**24-Hour Staffing:** All facilities must maintain at least **one awake staff member on duty 24 hours per day, 7 days per week**, capable of responding to resident needs and emergencies.

**Minimum Staffing:** While Illinois does not mandate specific staff-to-resident ratios for all situations, facilities must demonstrate staffing adequacy through resident assessments and service plans.

**Administrator Presence:** Administrators must be present during normal business hours to oversee operations, address concerns, and ensure regulatory compliance.

### Staff Qualifications and Training

**Direct Care Staff:**

**Age:** Minimum 18 years of age

**Education:** High school diploma or equivalent preferred but not required for all positions

**Training Requirements:**
- Orientation training before independent work with residents
- Job-specific training addressing responsibilities and competencies
- Ongoing training on regulatory requirements and best practices
- Specialized training for staff working with residents with dementia
- Medication administration training for staff administering medications
- Emergency preparedness and evacuation training
- Infection control and universal precautions
- Resident rights and abuse prevention
- First aid and CPR certification

**Background Screening:** All staff must complete criminal background checks. Disqualifying offenses include violent crimes, sexual offenses, theft, fraud, abuse, neglect, and exploitation.

**Medication Administration:** Unlicensed staff may administer medications only after completing approved medication administration training and demonstrating competency.

### Supportive Living Facility Staffing

**Enhanced Requirements:** SLFs must maintain staffing sufficient to meet the needs of residents requiring nursing home level of care.

**Licensed Nursing Staff:** SLFs must employ or contract with licensed nurses (RN or LPN) to:
- Conduct resident health assessments
- Develop and implement nursing care plans
- Oversee medication management
- Provide health monitoring and interventions
- Coordinate with physicians and healthcare providers
- Train and supervise direct care staff
- Ensure quality assurance and compliance

**Personal Care Staff:** SLFs must provide adequate personal care staff to deliver unlimited personal care services as needed by residents.

**24-Hour Awake Staff:** SLFs must maintain awake staff 24/7 capable of responding to resident needs and emergencies.

## Physical Plant Requirements

### Assisted Living Establishment Requirements

**Apartment-Style Units:** ALEs must provide **single-occupancy private apartment units** containing:

**Living Space:** Adequate square footage for living and sleeping areas (minimum requirements specified in regulations)

**Private Bathroom:** Each unit must contain private bathroom with:
- Toilet
- Sink
- Bathing facility (shower or tub)
- Grab bars and accessibility features
- Adequate lighting
- Ventilation

**Kitchenette:** Each unit must contain kitchenette with:
- Sink
- Refrigerator
- Cooking appliance (microwave or range)
- Storage for food and utensils

**Lockable Door:** Each unit must have lockable entrance door, with residents controlling access (staff may have emergency access)

**Climate Control:** Individual climate controls enabling residents to adjust temperature

**Emergency Call System:** Emergency call system accessible from living areas and bathroom

**Windows:** Natural lighting through windows

**Storage:** Adequate storage for personal belongings (closets, cabinets)

**Life Safety Code Compliance:** All facilities must comply with applicable NFPA Life Safety Code requirements including:
- Automatic fire detection systems
- Fire alarm systems
- Automatic fire sprinkler systems (required for most facilities)
- Fire extinguishers
- Emergency lighting and exit signage
- Egress routes with adequate width
- Fire-resistant construction

**Accessibility:** Facilities must comply with ADA accessibility standards and Illinois accessibility codes.

**Common Areas:** Facilities must provide adequate common spaces for:
- Dining
- Activities and recreation
- Socialization and visiting
- Outdoor spaces when feasible

### Supportive Living Facility Requirements

**Apartment Requirements:** SLFs must meet the same apartment-style requirements as ALEs, with emphasis on:
- Homelike environments
- Resident autonomy and control
- Privacy and dignity
- Community integration

**Physical Separation:** SLFs within mixed-use buildings must maintain distinct physical and operational separation from nursing homes or other healthcare facilities.

**Accessibility:** Enhanced accessibility features supporting residents with mobility limitations and disabilities.

## Service Requirements

### Admission Procedures

**Appropriateness Assessment:** Facilities may only admit residents who:
- Meet admission criteria for facility type
- Can be safely and appropriately served with available services
- Do not require continuous nursing care (intermittent nursing permitted)
- Are compatible with other residents and facility environment

**Pre-Admission Assessment:** Before admission, prospective residents must complete assessments evaluating:
- Current health status and diagnoses
- Functional abilities and limitations
- Cognitive status
- Medication needs
- Appropriateness for facility type
- Special needs or accommodations required

**SLP Eligibility:** For SLP admission, residents must:
- Be Medicaid-eligible
- Require nursing home level of care (as determined by assessment)
- Be appropriate for supportive living setting
- Choose SLP over nursing home placement

**Admission Agreements:** Facilities must execute written admission agreements covering services, fees, rights, responsibilities, and discharge criteria.

### Service Planning

**Individualized Service Plans:** Facilities must develop individualized service plans for each resident addressing:
- Personal care needs and preferences
- Medication management
- Healthcare coordination
- Social and recreational activities
- Dietary needs and preferences
- Special accommodations
- Goals and desired outcomes

**Service Plan Updates:** Service plans must be reviewed and updated regularly and when resident needs change.

**Resident Participation:** Residents and their representatives must be involved in service plan development and updates.

### Medication Management

**Medication Administration:** Facilities may assist residents with medications through:
- Self-administration with supervision
- Administration by licensed nurses
- Administration by trained unlicensed staff

**Medication Storage:** Secure medication storage with proper temperature control and organization

**Medication Documentation:** Accurate medication administration records

## Resident Rights and Protections

### Statutory Rights

Illinois law establishes comprehensive resident rights including:

**Autonomy and Choice:** Residents have the right to make decisions about their care, activities, and lifestyle

**Privacy and Dignity:** Residents have the right to privacy and to be treated with dignity and respect

**Communication and Visitation:** Residents have the right to communicate freely and receive visitors

**Freedom from Abuse:** Residents have the right to be free from abuse, neglect, and exploitation

**Participation in Care:** Residents have the right to participate in care planning

**Grievance Procedures:** Residents have the right to voice grievances without retaliation

### Complaint Process

**IDPH Complaint Hotline:** Residents, families, and others may file complaints with IDPH regarding suspected violations

**Investigation:** IDPH investigates all complaints with timelines based on severity

**Ombudsman Program:** Illinois's Long-Term Care Ombudsman Program provides independent advocacy
