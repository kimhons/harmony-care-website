# Kentucky Assisted Living Regulatory Overview

## Executive Summary

Kentucky regulates assisted living through the **Kentucky Office of Inspector General, Division of Health Care**, which licenses **Assisted Living Communities (ALCs)** with **three licensure options**: **Social Model**, **Basic Health Services (ALC-BH)**, and **Dementia Care (ALC-DC)**. Kentucky's regulatory framework is notable for its **private business entity model** with no public funding available, **ambulatory requirement** with temporary condition exceptions (6-month recovery window or hospice care), **three-tiered licensure system** allowing facilities to choose their service scope, **comprehensive medication management** restricted to ALC-BH and ALC-DC facilities with certified medication aide (CMA) delegation system approved by the Kentucky Board of Nursing, **high school diploma requirement** for administrators (no college degree required), **30-day orientation training** covering 11 comprehensive topics, **16 hours specialized dementia training** for ALC-DC staff (minimum 6 hours within first 60 days), **10 hours annual dementia-specific training** for ALC-DC managers/directors, **one awake staff member on-site at all times** requirement, **no numeric staffing ratios** (must be "sufficient" based on 24-hour resident needs per lease agreement, functional needs assessment, and service plan), **functional needs assessment** upon move-in and annually with standardized form, **quality management activity** with 2-year documentation retention, **comprehensive infection control policies**, and **detailed emergency disaster plans** with evacuation, sheltering, and missing resident procedures. Kentucky emphasizes flexibility through its three-tiered licensure model while maintaining strong medication management oversight and dementia care training standards.

## Facility Licensure

### Licensure Terms

Kentucky offers **three licensure options** for Assisted Living Communities:

1. **Social Model** - Basic assisted living services
2. **Basic Health Services (ALC-BH)** - Includes medication management
3. **Dementia Care (ALC-DC)** - Specialized dementia care with enhanced services

### Definition

"Assisted living community" means a licensed facility that provides **sleeping accommodations** and **assisted living services** set forth in the assisted living community's lease and policies for **5 or more adult persons** not related within the third degree of consanguinity to the owner or manager.

### Minimum Size

Facilities serving **5 or more adult persons** are licensed as Assisted Living Communities.

### Private Business Entity Model

Assisted living communities are considered **private business entities** and **no public funding is available** for services provided in this setting.

## Facility Scope of Care

The assisted living community shall provide each resident with access to the following services according to the lease agreement:

a) **Assistance with activities of daily living** and instrumental activities of daily living;
b) **Three meals and snacks** made available each day, with flexibility in a secured dementia care unit to meet the needs of residents with cognitive impairments who may eat outside of scheduled dining hours;
c) **Scheduled daily social activities** that address the general preferences of residents;
d) **Assistance with self-administration of medication**; and
e) **Housing**.

### Basic Health Services (ALC-BH)

The assisted living community may provide residents with access to **basic health and health-related services**. If an assisted living community chooses to provide basic health and health-related services, the assisted living community shall supervise the residents.

### Dementia Care (ALC-DC)

In addition to these minimum services required, an assisted living community with dementia care shall also provide:

a) **Assistance with activities of daily living** that address the needs of each resident with dementia;
b) **Nonpharmacological practices** that are person-centered and evidence-informed;
c) **Informational services** educating persons living with dementia and their legal and designated representatives about transitions in care and expectations of residents while in care;
d) **Social activities** offered on or off the premises of the licensed assisted living community with dementia care that provide residents with opportunities to engage with other residents and the broader community; and
e) **Basic health and health-related services**.

## Limitations of Services

### Ambulatory Requirement

A resident shall be **ambulatory** unless due to a **temporary condition**.

"**Ambulatory**" means able to walk, transfer, or move from place to place with or without hands-on assistance of another person, and with or without an assistive device, including but not limited to a walker or a wheelchair.

### Temporary Condition Exceptions

"**Temporary condition**" means a condition that affects a resident as follows:

**Option 1:** The resident is not ambulatory before or after entering a lease agreement with the assisted living community but is expected to regain ambulatory ability within **6 months** of loss of ambulation, as documented by a licensed health care professional, and the assisted living community has a written plan in place to mitigate risk; or

**Option 2:** The resident is not ambulatory after entering a lease agreement with the assisted living community but is not expected to regain ambulatory ability, **hospice services** are provided by a licensed hospice program licensed or other end-of-life services are provided by a licensed health care provider, as documented by a licensed hospice program or other licensed health care professional, and the assisted living community has a written plan in place to mitigate risk.

## Resident Assessment and Service Planning

### Functional Needs Assessment

The assisted living community shall complete a **functional needs assessment** for each resident and provide a copy to the resident upon **move-in** and **as needed** with updated information if there is a change in the resident's condition, but **no later than once every 12 months**. The assessment uses a **standardized form**.

The functional needs assessment shall be used to ensure that the prospective or current resident:

a) Meets the eligibility criteria;
b) Has at least minimal ability to verbally direct or physically participate in activities of daily living (ADL) or instrumental activities of daily living (IADL) during the time in which assistance is provided;
c) Is free from signs and symptoms of any communicable disease that is likely to be transmitted to other residents or staff;
d) Does not have any special dietary needs that the facility is unable to meet; and
e) Does not require 24 hour nursing supervision.

### Medical Examination

An assisted living community must require a **medical examination prior to admission** of a resident. An assisted living community must complete a functional needs assessment for each resident.

## Medication Management

### Restriction to Licensed Facilities

Medication administration is **restricted to communities licensed as ALC-BH and ALC-DC**.

### Self-Administration Assistance

The assisted living community provides **assistance with self-administration of medication** that is prepared or directed by the client, the client's designated representative, or a licensed health care professional who is not the owner, manager, or employee of the assisted living community.

### Medication Management Assessment

Prior to providing medication management services, the facility shall have a **nurse or other licensed health professional conduct an assessment** that shall:

a) Be face-to-face with the resident;
b) Determine what medication management services will be provided and how the services will be provided;
c) Include an identification and review of all medications the resident is known to be taking, including:
   - Indications for medications;
   - Side effects;
   - Contraindications; and
   - Possible allergic or adverse reactions, and actions to address these issues;
d) Identify interventions needed in the management of medications to prevent diversion of medication by the resident or others who may have access to the medications; and
e) Provide instructions to the resident and designated contacts or legal representatives on interventions to prevent diversion of medications, such as misuse, theft, or illegal or improper disposition of medications.

### Medication Management Record

For each resident receiving medication management services, the ALC-BH or ALC-DC shall develop and maintain a **current individualized medication management record** for each resident based on the resident's assessment.

### Medication Reconciliation

**Medication reconciliation** shall be completed by a nurse, licensed health professional acting within the professional's scope of practice, or authorized prescriber for each resident receiving medication management services.

### Delegation to Certified Medication Aides

A nurse or other appropriate licensed health professional may **delegate medication administration** to an unlicensed staff person in an ALC-BH or ALC-DC as follows:

**For oral or topical medication delegation:**
The unlicensed staff person shall have:
- **Certified medication aide I credential** from a training and skills competency evaluation program approved by the Kentucky Board of Nursing (KBN); or
- **Kentucky medication aide credential** from the Kentucky Community and Technical College System (KCTCS)

**For preloaded insulin injection delegation:**
If administration of a preloaded insulin injection is delegated in addition to oral or topical medication, the unlicensed staff person shall have a **certified medication aide II credential** from a training and skills competency evaluation program approved by the Kentucky Board of Nursing (KBN).

### Nurse Availability

An ALC-BH or ALC-DC shall ensure that a **nurse or licensed health professional is readily available** in person, by telephone, or by other means of live, two-way communication to unlicensed staff at times the staff is providing delegated nursing tasks.

### Controlled Substances

**Controlled substances** shall be kept under **double lock** (for example, stored in a locked box in a locked cabinet), and keys or access codes to the locked box and locked cabinet shall be accessible to designated staff only.

A nurse may delegate administration of a regularly scheduled controlled substance to a **certified medication aide (CMA)** if the medication has been prescribed and labeled in a container for a specific resident.

For a controlled substance ordered on a PRN basis, a nurse may delegate administration to a CMA if:
- The medication has been prescribed and labeled in a container for a specific resident;
- The nurse assesses the resident, in person or virtually, prior to administration of the PRN controlled substance;
- The nurse assesses the resident, in person or virtually, following the administration of the PRN controlled substance; and
- The nurse documents administration of the PRN controlled substance by a CMA in the resident's record.

### Controlled Substances Record Keeping

There shall be a **controlled substances bound record book** with numbered pages that includes:
- Name of the resident;
- Date, time, kind, dosage, and method of administration of each controlled substance;
- Name of the practitioner who prescribed the medications;
- Name of the nurse or CMA who administered the controlled substance or provided assistance with self-administration

An appropriately authorized licensed health professional with access to controlled substances shall be responsible for maintaining a recorded and signed:
- **Schedule II controlled substances count daily**; and
- **Schedule III, IV, and V controlled substances count at least 1 time per week**.

## Administrator Requirements

### Qualifications

An assisted living community shall have a **designated manager** who is at least **21 years of age**, has at least a **high school diploma or a High School Equivalency Diploma**, and has demonstrated **management or administrative ability** to maintain the daily operations.

### No Degree Requirement

Kentucky does **not require** a college degree for assisted living administrators, only a high school diploma or equivalent.

## Staffing Requirements

### 24/7 Awake Staff Requirement

**One awake staff member** shall be on site at each licensed entity at **all times**.

### Sufficient Staffing Based on Resident Needs

Staffing in an assisted living community shall be **sufficient in number and qualification** to meet the **24 hour scheduled needs** of each resident pursuant to the lease agreement, functional needs assessment, and service plan.

### No Numeric Staffing Ratios

Kentucky does **not specify numeric staffing ratios**. Facilities must maintain staffing that is "sufficient" based on resident needs as documented in assessments and service plans.

### Nurse Availability for Medication Management

An ALC-BH and ALC-DC shall have a **nurse readily available** in person, by telephone, or by other means of live, two-way communication to unlicensed staff at times the staff is providing delegated nursing tasks.

### Emergency Evacuation Assistance

When a resident requires hands-on assistance of another person to walk, transfer, or move from place to place with or without an assistive device, the assisted living community shall have a policy that describes how **priority will be given by staff sufficient to assist that resident during times of emergency** when evacuation may be necessary.

## Direct Care Staff Training Requirements

### 30-Day Orientation Training

Prior to working independently with residents and **within 30 days from the date of hire**, all staff and management shall receive **orientation education** that addresses the topics with emphasis on those most applicable to the employee's assigned duties:

a) **Resident rights**;
b) **Community policies**;
c) **Adult first aid**;
d) **Cardiopulmonary resuscitation** unless the policies of the assisted living community state that this procedure is not initiated by its staff, and that residents and prospective residents are informed of the policies;
e) **Adult abuse and neglect**;
f) **Alzheimer's disease and other types of dementia**;
g) **Emergency procedures**;
h) **Aging process**;
i) **Assistance with activities of daily living and instrumental activities of daily living**;
j) **Particular needs or conditions** if the assisted living community markets itself as providing special programming, staffing, or training on behalf of residents with particular needs or conditions; and
k) **Assistance with self-administration of medication**.

### Annual Training

All staff and management shall receive **annual training** applicable to their assigned duties that addresses **no fewer than 4 of the topics listed**, **1 of which shall be Alzheimer's disease and other types of dementia**.

### Dementia Care Training Requirements

#### ALC-DC Manager/Director Training

The manager or director of an **ALC-DC** shall complete at least **10 hours of annual dementia-specific training**.

#### ALC-DC Direct Care Staff Training

All direct care staff who work in the facility's **secured dementia care unit** staff shall receive at least **8 hours of dementia-specific orientation** within the first 30 days of working in the secured dementia care unit. Until this initial training is complete, an employee shall not provide direct care unless there is another employee on site who has completed the initial 8 hours of training on topics related to dementia care and who can act as a resource and assist as needed.

The orientation shall include:
a) Information about the nature, progression, and management of Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders;
b) Methods for creating an environment that minimizes challenging behavior from residents with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders;
c) Methods for identifying and minimizing safety risks to residents with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders; and
d) Methods for communicating with individuals with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders.

#### ALC-DC Comprehensive Dementia Training

All direct care staff members an ALC-DC shall also receive orientation training within the first 30 days of caring for residents that includes at a minimum:

**General training:**
- Development and implementation of comprehensive and individual service plans;
- Skills for recognizing physical and cognitive changes in residents;
- General infection control principles; and
- Emergency preparedness training

**Specialized training in dementia care:**
- The nature of Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders;
- The unit's philosophy related to the care of residents with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders;
- The unit's policies and procedures related to the care of residents with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders;
- Behavioral problems commonly found in residents with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders;
- Positive therapeutic interventions and activities;
- Skills for maintaining the safety of the residents; and
- The role of family in caring for residents with Alzheimer's and other dementia illnesses and disorders.

Direct care staff an ALC-DC shall complete a minimum of **16 hours of specialized training in dementia care** (which must include **6 hours approved by the Office of Dementia Services within the first 60 days**) and a minimum of **8 hours of specialized training in dementia care annually thereafter**.

## Quality Requirements

Each ALC, ALC-BH, or ALC-DC shall engage in **quality management activity** appropriate to the size of the facility and relevant to the type of services provided.

"**Quality management activity**" means evaluating the quality of care by:
a) Reviewing resident services, complaints made, and other issues that have occurred; and
b) Determining if changes in services, staffing, or other procedures need to be made to ensure safe and competent services to residents.

Documentation about the facility's quality management activity shall be:
a) **Maintained for at least 2 years**; and
b) **Available to the Office of Inspector General** at the time of the survey, investigation, or renewal.

## Infection Control Requirements

All direct care staff members shall receive **orientation training** that includes **general infection control principles**.

Each ALC, ALC-BH, and ALC-DC shall maintain **written policies and procedures** that are up-to-date and include infection control practices that address:
- The prevention of disease transmission; and
- Cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization methods used for equipment and the environment.

## Emergency Preparedness

Each ALC, ALC-BH, and ALC-DC shall have a **written emergency disaster plan** that:

1. **Contains a plan for evacuation**, including how priority will be given to assist a resident during an evacuation who requires hands-on assistance from another person to walk, transfer, or move from place to place with or without an assistive device;
2. **Addresses elements of sheltering in place** or provides instructions for finding a safe location indoors and staying there until given an all clear or told to evacuate;
3. **Identifies temporary relocation sites**; and
4. **Details staff assignments** in the event of a disaster or an emergency.

### Emergency Plan Requirements

Each ALC, ALC-BH, and ALC-DC shall:
1. **Post an emergency disaster plan prominently**;
2. **Provide building emergency exit diagrams** to all residents;
3. **Post emergency exit diagrams on each floor**; and
4. **Have a written policy and procedure** regarding missing tenant residents.
5. **Provide emergency and disaster training** to all staff during the initial staff orientation and annually;
6. **Make emergency and disaster training available to residents annually**.

Staff who have not received emergency and disaster training shall work only if staff trained for emergencies and disaster are also working on site.

### Disaster Planning Reference

Disaster planning and emergency preparedness plan requirements are detailed in **KRS 194A.717(5)** and include evacuation plans, sheltering instructions, temporary relocation sites, and staff assignments.

## Medicaid and Financial Assistance

### No Public Funding

Assisted living communities are considered **private business entities** and **no public funding is available** for services provided in this setting.

Kentucky does not have a Medicaid waiver program specifically for assisted living communities.

### Medical Examination

An assisted living community must require a **medical examination prior to admission** of a resident.

### Functional Needs Assessment

An assisted living community must provide each resident with a **copy of the resident's rights**.

## Key Distinguishing Features of Kentucky Regulations

### 1. Three-Tiered Licensure System

Kentucky offers three distinct licensure options (Social Model, ALC-BH, ALC-DC), allowing facilities to choose their service scope and regulatory requirements.

### 2. No Public Funding Model

Kentucky explicitly designates assisted living communities as private business entities with no public funding available, creating a purely private-pay market.

### 3. Ambulatory Requirement with Temporary Condition Exceptions

Kentucky requires residents to be ambulatory unless experiencing a temporary condition with either a 6-month recovery window or hospice care, providing flexibility while maintaining safety standards.

### 4. Certified Medication Aide System

Kentucky utilizes a comprehensive CMA credentialing system approved by the Kentucky Board of Nursing, with CMA I for oral/topical medications and CMA II for preloaded insulin injections.

### 5. High School Diploma Administrator Requirement

Kentucky requires only a high school diploma or equivalent for administrators, not requiring a college degree, making administrator positions more accessible.

### 6. Comprehensive 30-Day Orientation Training

Kentucky mandates orientation training covering 11 comprehensive topics within 30 days of hire, ensuring thorough staff preparation before independent work with residents.

### 7. Extensive Dementia Care Training Requirements

ALC-DC facilities must provide 8 hours of dementia-specific orientation within 30 days, 16 hours total specialized dementia training (including 6 hours approved by Office of Dementia Services within first 60 days), and 8 hours annual dementia training thereafter.

### 8. 10 Hours Annual Dementia Training for ALC-DC Managers

Managers/directors of dementia care facilities must complete 10 hours of annual dementia-specific training, ensuring leadership maintains current dementia care expertise.

### 9. No Numeric Staffing Ratios

Kentucky does not specify numeric staffing ratios, instead requiring "sufficient" staffing based on 24-hour resident needs per lease agreement, functional needs assessment, and service plan.

### 10. Controlled Substances Double Lock Requirement

Kentucky mandates double lock storage for controlled substances (locked box in locked cabinet) with daily counts for Schedule II and weekly counts for Schedule III-V substances.
