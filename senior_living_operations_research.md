# Senior Living Operations Research - Key Findings

## Core Operational Departments

Based on industry research, senior living facilities operate across 8 core departments:

1. **Concierge and Reception Operations**
2. **Resident Relations and Health Operations**
3. **Maintenance and Facility Improvements Operations**
4. **Activities Operations**
5. **Food and Beverage Operations**
6. **Housekeeping Operations**
7. **Laundry Operations**
8. **Transportation Operations**

## Facility Types and Care Levels

### Independent Living Facilities
- Least care assistance
- Townhomes, condominiums, or single-family homes
- Groundskeeping and building maintenance covered
- Fewer staffing needs

### Assisted Living Facilities
- Personal-care amenities (food services, laundry, property maintenance)
- Assistance with daily activities (bathing, dressing, meal preparation)
- No regular nursing care required

### Memory Care
- Specialized care for Alzheimer's and dementia patients
- Higher safety requirements
- Medically trained staff or nurses on site
- Additional safety protocols for wandering prevention

## Key Operational Challenges

1. **High Churn Rates**
   - Employee turnover: 50%
   - Resident turnover: 47%
   - Average length of stay: 22-29 months

2. **Staffing Issues**
   - Industry understaffed
   - High growth leading to personnel shortages
   - Need for specialized training

3. **Technology Gap**
   - Many facilities still using pen and paper
   - Radio communications
   - Need for digital transformation

4. **Safety and Security**
   - Risk prevention critical
   - Protocols for wet floors, viral outbreaks
   - Wandering prevention for memory care
   - Emergency preparedness

## Resident Relations Best Practices

- Welcome letters with important information
- Regular resident opinion surveys
- Immediate complaint resolution
- Resident council (6+ elected residents)
- Health initiatives (immunization, physical exams)
- Emergency planning and advance directives
- Wellness programs

## Maintenance Operations

**Resident-Facing Assets**:
- Boilers
- HVAC systems
- In-room safety equipment
- Chillers
- Amenities (pool, gym)

**Staff Equipment**:
- Laundry machines
- Food storage and preparation equipment

**Maintenance Types**:
- Preventative maintenance (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, annually)
- Work order system for resident requests
- Asset tracking and historical logging

- Aquatic fitness, swimming lessons

- Dance classes, meditation groups

## Housekeeping Operations

**Responsibilities**:
- Vacuuming resident quarters
- Wiping and sanitizing surfaces
- Cleaning bathroom fixtures
- Notifying staff of resident health changes

**Frequency**:
- Regular room visits
- Deep cleaning schedules
- Common area cleaning

## Laundry Operations

**In-House Pros**:
- Quick transportation
- Readily available linens
- Reduced loss/theft

**In-House Cons**:
- High equipment cost
- Space requirements
- Need for qualified staff

**Outsourced**:
- Cost savings on equipment/staffing
- Charged by the pound
- Higher risk of loss/theft
- Quality compromise

## Transportation Services

**Purpose**:
- Grocery shopping
- Independent errands
- Doctor visits
- Religious services
- Social outings

**Challenges**:
- Many residents no longer drive
- Scheduling complexity
- Vehicle maintenance
- Driver availability
