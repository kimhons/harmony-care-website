# Top 100 Pain Points in Residential Care Facilities

## Category 1: Staffing & Workforce (20 pain points)

1. **Catastrophic staff turnover** - 53% annual turnover rate, double that of hospitals
2. **Chronic staff shortages** - Lost 200,000+ workers since pandemic, workforce at 1994 levels
3. **Difficulty recruiting direct-care workers** - Health professionals unwilling to work in LTC
4. **Excessive workload** - Staff forced into extra shifts and overtime
5. **Inadequate wages and benefits** - Poor compensation vs other healthcare sectors
6. **Lack of training opportunities** - Insufficient ongoing education for staff
7. **Limited career advancement** - No clear path for professional growth
8. **Management issues** - Poor leadership and supervision
9. **Staff burnout** - Administrative burden and excessive workloads
10. **Reliance on expensive agency staff** - Skyrocketing temporary staffing costs
11. **Running below ideal staffing quotas** - Unable to meet minimum staffing requirements
12. **Substitute staff with no experience** - High turnover leads to untrained replacements
13. **Lack of interest in LTC work** - Stigma associated with nursing home employment
14. **Insufficient delegation training** - Non-licensed staff inadequately prepared
15. **Poor staff retention** - Inability to keep experienced workers
16. **Lack of continuity of care** - Constant staff changes disrupt resident relationships
17. **Inadequate supervision** - Registered nurses unable to oversee all shifts
18. **Weekend and night staffing gaps** - No RN availability during off-hours
19. **Staff dissatisfaction** - Low morale and engagement
20. **Difficulty finding specialized staff** - Shortage of nurses, therapists, and specialists

## Category 2: Financial & Revenue Cycle (15 pain points)

21. **Negative operating margins** - Average -17% operating margin across sector
22. **More than 50% operating at a loss** - Unsustainable financial position
23. **Low occupancy rates** - Below 80%, reducing revenue
24. **Inadequate Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement** - Payments don't cover costs
25. **Loss of pandemic funding** - Emergency Provider Relief Funds ended
26. **45% can't sustain losses beyond 1 year** - Imminent closure risk
27. **579 nursing homes closed since pandemic** - Sector consolidation
28. **Billing errors and missed charges** - Revenue leakage from poor documentation
29. **Complex insurance prior authorizations** - Delays in payment approval
30. **Cash flow challenges** - Delayed reimbursements impact operations
31. **Accounts management inefficiencies** - Poor AR/AP processes
32. **High cost of compliance** - Regulatory requirements expensive to meet
33. **Increasing reporting costs** - Administrative burden of documentation
34. **Reduced bed capacity** - 45,217 fewer beds since pandemic
35. **21% downsized due to labor shortages** - Forced to reduce services

## Category 3: Medication Management (15 pain points)

36. **Medication administration errors** - Wrong drug, dose, or timing
37. **Lack of pharmacology knowledge** - Staff unable to identify side effects
38. **Delegation to non-licensed staff** - Unqualified personnel administering drugs
39. **Incomprehensible prescription documents** - Handwritten, unclear prescriptions
40. **Generic drug substitution confusion** - Multiple names for same substance
41. **Failure to monitor side effects** - Staff not trained to recognize adverse reactions
42. **Drug interaction conflicts** - Polypharmacy creates dangerous combinations
43. **Allergy conflicts** - Medications given despite known allergies
44. **PRN medication mismanagement** - As-needed drugs given incorrectly
45. **Prescription errors** - Wrong medications prescribed
46. **Dispensation errors** - Pharmacy errors in filling prescriptions
47. **Missing medications** - Tablets lost or unaccounted for
48. **Not controlling dosages** - Staff failing to verify tablet counts
49. **Relying on memory vs. written records** - Dangerous shortcuts
50. **Multiple medication systems** - Pill organizers + pharmacy management causing confusion

## Category 4: Compliance & Regulatory (15 pain points)

51. **Excessive regulatory burden** - Overwhelming documentation requirements
52. **Infection control violations** - Most common deficiency cited
53. **Failure to provide necessary care** - Substandard health inspection ratings
54. **Accident hazards not addressed** - Facility safety violations
55. **Food safety violations** - Improper storage/preparation/distribution
56. **Inadequate care plans** - Plans not comprehensive or individualized
57. **Services don't meet professional standards** - Quality of care issues
58. **Clinical records don't meet standards** - Documentation deficiencies
59. **Employing persons guilty of abuse** - Background check failures
60. **Unnecessary drug regimens** - Overmedication and inappropriate prescribing
61. **Dignity violations** - Resident rights not respected
62. **Substantial fines and compliance costs** - Penalties for violations
63. **Increased substantiated complaints** - More resident/family grievances
64. **Inspection preparation burden** - Time-intensive compliance activities
65. **Risk management complexity** - Difficult to track and mitigate risks

## Category 5: Clinical Care & Quality (10 pain points)

66. **Increased resident abuse instances** - Correlated with high turnover
67. **Inability to assess medication risks** - Staff lack clinical judgment
68. **Poor care coordination** - Fragmented services across disciplines
69. **Inadequate resident assessments** - Incomplete or inaccurate evaluations
70. **Failure to update care plans** - Plans become outdated and irrelevant
71. **Lack of person-centered care** - Cookie-cutter approaches vs. individualized
72. **Insufficient therapy services** - PT/OT/ST not available or adequate
73. **Poor wound care management** - Pressure ulcers and infections
74. **Inadequate pain management** - Residents suffering unnecessarily
75. **Mental health needs unmet** - Depression, anxiety, dementia care gaps

## Category 6: Technology & Systems (10 pain points)

76. **EHR adoption barriers** - High costs, complexity, resistance to change
77. **Lack of interoperability** - Systems don't communicate with each other
78. **Too few computers** - Insufficient hardware for staff
79. **Limited/slow internet access** - Poor connectivity infrastructure
80. **Lack of wireless connectivity** - Mobility constraints for staff
81. **Poor system integration** - Multiple disconnected platforms
82. **Inadequate IT support** - No technical assistance when problems arise
83. **Up to 70% technology implementation failures** - Projects don't achieve goals
84. **Staff workarounds** - Technology doesn't fit workflow, staff bypass it
85. **High maintenance costs** - Ongoing expenses for systems upkeep

## Category 7: Family Engagement & Communication (8 pain points)

86. **Poor family communication** - Lack of proactive updates to families
87. **Family dissatisfaction** - Complaints about care quality and responsiveness
88. **Lack of family involvement in care planning** - Families excluded from decisions
89. **Difficulty reaching staff** - Phone calls and messages go unanswered
90. **Conflicting information** - Different staff members provide contradictory updates
91. **No transparency** - Families kept in the dark about incidents
92. **Inadequate visitor accommodations** - Uncomfortable or restrictive visiting policies
93. **Language barriers** - Communication challenges with non-English speaking families

## Category 8: Operations & Administration (7 pain points)

94. **Excessive paperwork** - Manual, time-consuming administrative tasks
95. **Data entry requirements** - Redundant documentation in multiple systems
96. **Complex billing and coding** - Revenue cycle inefficiencies
97. **Supply chain and procurement issues** - Inventory management challenges
98. **Payroll and HR management complexity** - Time-consuming manual processes
99. **Customer support process inefficiencies** - Slow response to resident/family needs
100.  **Underreporting of incidents** - Safety events not documented or analyzed

## Impact Severity Ratings

### Critical (Immediate Risk to Life/License):

- Medication administration errors (#36-50)
- Infection control violations (#52)
- Resident abuse (#66)
- Negative operating margins (#21-35)

### High (Significant Impact on Quality/Viability):

- Staff turnover and shortages (#1-20)
- Compliance violations (#51-65)
- Clinical care gaps (#67-75)

### Medium (Operational Inefficiency):

- Technology adoption barriers (#76-85)
- Family communication issues (#86-93)
- Administrative burden (#94-100)
