# Facility Health Assessment Tool

## Interactive Self-Assessment for Residential Care Facilities

---

## Instructions

This assessment helps you identify your facility's strengths and opportunities for improvement across six critical dimensions. Rate each statement on a scale of 1-5:

**1 = Critical (Immediate action required)**  
**2 = Poor (Significant improvement needed)**  
**3 = Fair (Some improvement needed)**  
**4 = Good (Minor improvements possible)**  
**5 = Excellent (Best-in-class performance)**

Be honest in your assessment. This tool is designed to help you, not judge you.

## Scoring Interpretation

### 120-150 Points: THRIVING

**Congratulations!** Your facility is performing at a high level across all dimensions. You're in the top quartile of the industry.

**Recommendations:**

- Document and share your best practices
- Consider mentoring struggling facilities
- Focus on continuous improvement and innovation
- Explore opportunities to expand services or capacity

**Opportunities:**
Even thriving facilities can benefit from:

- Advanced analytics for predictive insights
- Automation to free up staff time for strategic initiatives
- Benchmarking against other top performers

---

### 90-119 Points: STABLE

**Good work!** Your facility is performing adequately with opportunities for improvement in specific areas.

**Recommendations:**

- Identify your 3 lowest-scoring sections
- Prioritize improvements in those areas
- Invest in targeted solutions (technology, training, process improvement)
- Set measurable goals for the next 6-12 months

**Focus Areas:**

- Sections scoring below 15 points need immediate attention
- Look for quick wins that can build momentum
- Consider technology solutions to address multiple pain points simultaneously

---

### 60-89 Points: STRUGGLING

**Action needed.** Your facility is facing significant challenges that require systematic intervention.

**Recommendations:**

- Conduct a comprehensive operational assessment
- Develop a 12-month transformation plan
- Secure leadership buy-in and resources
- Consider engaging external consultants or technology partners
- Address financial sustainability as top priority

**Critical Actions:**

- Sections scoring below 10 points are in crisis
- You likely need help - don't try to fix everything alone
- Focus on stopping the bleeding first (financial, staffing, compliance)
- Then build toward sustainable improvement

---

### Below 60 Points: CRISIS

**Immediate action required.** Your facility is at serious risk and needs urgent intervention.

**Recommendations:**

- Convene emergency leadership meeting
- Assess financial viability and runway
- Identify immediate compliance risks
- Consider bringing in turnaround specialists
- Explore comprehensive technology solutions
- May need to consider merger, acquisition, or closure if situation cannot be stabilized

**Critical Actions:**

- Any section scoring below 5 points is an immediate threat
- You cannot fix this alone - seek help immediately
- Focus on survival first: avoid closure, prevent harm, maintain compliance
- Develop realistic assessment of whether facility can be saved

## Section-Specific Insights

### If Staffing & Workforce is your lowest score:

**Root Cause:** Likely driven by compensation, workload, or culture issues

**Solutions:**

- Implement workforce optimization technology to reduce overtime
- Automate administrative tasks to improve work-life balance
- Enhance training and career development programs
- Consider wage increases (ROI: reduced turnover pays for itself)

---

### If Financial Performance is your lowest score:

**Root Cause:** Likely driven by low occupancy, high costs, or revenue leakage

**Solutions:**

- Conduct revenue cycle audit to identify billing errors
- Implement occupancy management strategies
- Reduce agency staffing through better scheduling
- Optimize payer mix and reimbursement capture

---

### If Clinical Quality is your lowest score:

**Root Cause:** Likely driven by inadequate systems, training, or oversight

**Solutions:**

- Implement clinical decision support technology
- Enhance medication safety protocols (barcode scanning, interaction checking)
- Improve care planning processes
- Increase clinical supervision and monitoring

---

### If Compliance & Regulatory is your lowest score:

**Root Cause:** Likely driven by documentation gaps or process failures

**Solutions:**

- Implement continuous compliance monitoring
- Automate documentation and reporting
- Conduct mock surveys quarterly
- Focus on top 10 deficiency prevention

---

### If Operations & Technology is your lowest score:

**Root Cause:** Likely driven by outdated systems or poor integration

**Solutions:**

- Invest in modern care management platform
- Prioritize system integration to eliminate duplicate entry
- Implement mobile technology for point-of-care documentation
- Provide comprehensive staff training and support

---

### If Family Engagement is your lowest score:

**Root Cause:** Likely driven by poor communication or lack of transparency

**Solutions:**

- Implement family portal for 24/7 access
- Automate family updates and notifications
- Increase transparency around care delivery
- Develop proactive complaint resolution process
