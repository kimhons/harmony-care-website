# Residential Care Facility Pain Points Research

## Source 1: Top 4 Pain Points for U.S. Nursing Home Providers (2026)

**URL:** https://pharmbills.com/blog/the-4-top-pain-points-for-u-s-nursing-home-providers

### Key Statistics:

- National net margin fell to -2.86% in 2022
- Average operating margin: -17% across the sector (2023)
- 579 nursing homes closed since pandemic
- Staff turnover: 53% (double that of hospitals)
- Lost 200,000+ workers since pandemic
- Workforce at 1994 levels, recovery expected until 2027
- 21% of facilities downsized beds/units due to labor shortages
- 45,217 fewer nursing home beds since pandemic
- More than 50% of nursing homes operating at a loss
- 45% can't sustain current pace of loss for more than 1 year
- Occupancy rates below 80% (Dec 2023)

### Pain Point Categories:

#### 1. High Staff Turnover (53%)

**Impacts:**

- Increased resident abuse instances
- Higher staff dissatisfaction
- Substandard health inspection ratings
- More substantiated complaints
- Substantial fines and compliance costs

**Root Causes:**

- Poor wages and benefits vs other healthcare sectors
- Lack of training opportunities
- Management issues
- Limited career advancement
- Excessive workloads
- Administrative burden causing burnout

#### 2. Ongoing Staff Shortages ("Catastrophic Crisis")

**Impacts:**

- Increased workload for existing staff
- Forced extra shifts and overtime
- Running below ideal staffing quotas
- Skyrocketing staffing costs
- Reliance on expensive agency staff
- Reduced bed capacity

**Challenges:**

- Difficulty recruiting direct-care workers
- Health professionals unwilling to work in LTC
- Vicious cycle of shortages

#### 3. Increasing Financial Pressures

**Factors:**

- Loss of pandemic funding (Emergency Provider Relief Funds)
- Low occupancy rates (below 80%)
- Increasing reporting and regulatory costs
- Inadequate Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement
- Increased staffing costs
- Operating losses unsustainable beyond 1 year

#### 4. Shift to Community-Based Care

**Trends:**

- Patients choosing home health care over facility-based care
- Medicare/Medicaid covered community programs (PACE)
- Declining occupancy levels
- Consumer preference for patient experience over traditional factors

**New Consumer Priorities:**

- Personalization of care
- Access and convenience
- Quality of experience
- Technology integration

## Source 2: Medication Management Errors in Nursing Homes (Research Study)

**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8082477/

### Key Findings on Medication Errors:

#### Human Factor Errors:

**Lack of Competence and Experience:**

- Insufficient delegation training programs
- Lack of knowledge about pharmacodynamics and anatomy/physiology
- Inability to relate symptoms to medication effects
- Non-licensed staff unable to assess medication risks
- Missing understanding of drug side effects

**Unclear Cooperation and Communication:**

- Uncertainty about who is responsible for what tasks
- Miscommunication between registered nurses and non-licensed staff
- Confusion about whether medications were administered
- Lack of communication between team members
- Different documentation systems creating barriers

**Unprofessional Approach:**

- Carelessness in checking dosages
- Not controlling tablet counts against prescriptions
- Administering already-given medications
- Relying on memory instead of written records
- Failing to note prescription changes
- Trusting colleagues without supervision

#### Technology/Tool Errors:

**Incomprehensible Prescription Documents:**

- Increased errors after new prescription card introduction
- Multiple prescriptions from different physicians causing confusion
- Handwritten changes being unclear
- Temporary drug prescriptions causing mix-ups
- Multiple medication systems (pill organizer + pharmacy management)

**Exchanges of Pharmaceutical Drugs:**

- Generic drug substitutions causing confusion
- One substance with many different product names
- Difficulty tracking replacement drugs
- Hospital discharge medication changes not communicated

#### Organizational Errors:

**Not Following Instructions:**

- Pressure to quickly obtain delegation to relieve workload
- Delegation becoming administrative task vs. safety priority
- Non-trained staff administering drugs due to staffing shortages
- Medications given in wrong locations
- Staff not staying to ensure medication was taken

**Irrational Working Conditions:**

- Interruptions during medication administration
- Insufficient time to complete delegated tasks safely
- Doses given too closely together due to delays
- Substitute staff with little/no experience
- Lack of interest in working in nursing homes among some staff

**Underreporting Incidents:**

- Lack of time to write incident reports
- Reports not written during spare time
- Incidents near end of shift not reported
- Severe mistakes not reported based on staff judgment
- No follow-up or analysis of reported incidents
- Lack of knowledge on how to write proper reports

### Impact Statistics:

- Medication errors are increasing in nursing homes
- Errors can lead to potentially life-threatening harm
- Polypharmacy common among aging population
- Complex medication regimens place elderly at risk

### Root Causes Identified:

1. Delegation of drug administration to non-licensed staff
2. Abandonment of routines
3. Carelessness
4. Lack of knowledge
5. Inadequate verbal communication
6. Lack of understanding of drug handling difficulties
7. Inability to control delegation
8. Standard of education issues
9. Safety awareness gaps
10. Lack of staff and time to act safely

### Medication Error Types:

- Wrong drug administered
- Wrong dosage given
- Medications given at wrong time
- Failure to monitor side effects
- Drug interaction conflicts
- Allergy conflicts
- PRN (as-needed) medication mismanagement
- Prescription errors
- Dispensation errors
- Administration errors
