## Design Specifications

### Visual Style:

- **Color Palette:**
  - Primary: Harmony teal (#0EA5E9)
  - Secondary: Warm coral (#F97316)
  - Accent: Deep purple (#7C3AED)
  - Neutrals: Charcoal (#1F2937), Light gray (#F3F4F6)

- **Typography:**
  - Headlines: Bold, modern sans-serif (Montserrat Bold)
  - Body: Clean, readable (Inter Regular)
  - Callouts: Medium weight (Inter Medium)

- **Photography:**
  - Authentic, diverse residents and staff
  - Modern facilities with technology
  - Warm, hopeful, professional tone
  - NO stock photos of sad elderly people

- **Infographics:**
  - Data visualization for all statistics
  - Icons for each pain point category
  - Charts and graphs for financial data
  - Timeline visualizations

### QR Code Strategy:

- **Placement:** Bottom right corner of every page
- **Size:** 1" x 1" (easily scannable)
- **Unique URLs:** Each QR code tracks which page drove the lead
- **Landing Pages:** Custom pages for each QR code with relevant content
- **Tracking:** UTM parameters for attribution

### Print Specifications:

- **Size:** 8.5" x 11" (standard letter)
- **Paper:** 100lb glossy cover stock
- **Binding:** Saddle-stitch
- **Finish:** Spot UV on cover
- **Print Run:** 10,000 copies initial

## Appendix: Alternative Magazine Concepts

### Concept B: "The Survival Guide"

- More urgent, crisis-focused tone
- Emphasis on avoiding closure
- 15 pages instead of 20
- Lower production cost

### Concept C: "The Innovation Report"

- Industry trends and future of care
- Thought leadership positioning
- Less product-focused
- 25 pages, more editorial

### Concept D: "The ROI Blueprint"

- Purely financial focus
- CFO/owner audience
- Heavy on numbers and calculations
- 12 pages, data-driven

**Recommendation:** Proceed with primary concept (Concept A) as it balances urgency, solution-focus, and credibility while appealing to multiple decision-makers.
